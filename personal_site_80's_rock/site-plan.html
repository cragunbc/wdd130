Content

Home page

80’S rock – Music that defined a generation 80’s rock formally known as classic rock has become some of the most recognizable music to ever been played. A lot of the music is still played on radios and sporting events to this day. Songs like Don’t Stop Believin’, Livin’ on a Prayer, Pour Some Sugar on Me among many others were the catalysts that launched 80’s rock to the top of the music industry and why the music is so popular still to this day. But what made these songs different?

What made 80’s rock different? Unlike prior music eras the 1980’s brought a different sound. It was a harder sound that relied heavier on the guitar. It introduces the “Power Ballad” which consisted of heavy drumming and recognizable guitar solos. Along with the power ballad becoming more widely used the progression of technology increased. Sound systems and synthesizers were cheaper to make and were able to be carried around easier from place to place making the music have a stronger foothold with its listeners.

Why is 80’s Rock still popular today? In a large way the music that was made in the 1980’s relayed a message to people’s personal lives. They connected with it and still care those feelings and messages with them today. A lot of movies and shows that have come out in recent years have used 80’s music or cared the same vibe that was present in the 1980’s. Those shows and movies reconnect a growing generation of people to their past and exposing a new generation to previously widely loved music.

Journey

Who is Journey? Journey was a band that was formally made in 1973 in San Francisco, California, but rose to the top during the 1980’s with a number of their electric songs. During their rise to the top their band consisted of 5 band members: Steve Perry (Vocal), Neal Schon (Guitar), Jonathan Cain (Piano), Steve Smith (Drums), and Ross Valory (Bass Guitar). Neal Schon who is the only founding member left of the band dropped out of High School and joined a band called Santana. They eventually broke off from Santana and made their own sound and music and called it Journey

Top 5 Songs 1. Don’t Stop Believin' 2. Faithfully 3. Any Way You Want It 4. Open Arms 5. Wheel in the Sky

What made Journey Successful? The most recognizable features of Journey were their guitar solos and their vocals. Steve Perry who formally has been giving the nickname “The Voice” was one of the driving forces that propelled them to many sold out stadiums and countless hits. Partnering that with Neal Schon’s guitar skills and it was a recipe for success. A lot of their success came from 2 of the albums that the released in the 80’s in Escape and Frontiers. These 2 albums were headlined by their top 2 best songs ever written in Don’t Stop Believin’ and Faithfully. Their album Escape became #1 in the nation after its release. They have since been inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

Bon Jovi

Who is Bon Jovi? Bon Jovi was a band that was formed in 1983 in Sayreville, New Jersey. During their rise to the top, they were composed of the following band members: Bon Jovi (Vocals), Richie Sambora (Guitar), Alec John Such (Bass), David Bryan (Keyboards), and Tico Torres (Drums). They were a local group of artists that grew up in the Sayreville area. When one of Jon’s solo songs “Runaway” hit the radio for the first time it took off, and with it came the formal formation of the band.

Top 5 Songs 1. Livin’ on a Prayer 2. Wanted Dead or Alive 3. Runaway 4. Bad Medicine 5. You Give Love a Bad Name

What made Bon Jovi Successful? When it comes to commercial success, they rose to the top with over 100 million albums sold helping form some of the most noticeable songs ever. Just a few years after their formation they released an album called Slippery When Wet and it was headlined by their most recognizable song in Livin’ on a Prayer. They followed other bands when it came to visual effects and lighting on stage that helped capture the attention of the audience and sell out multiple stadiums. They have since been inducted into the Hall of Fame.
